A Study of Street-level Navigation Techniques in 3D Digital Cities on Mobile Touch Devices

To characterize currently most common interaction techniques for street-level navigation in 3D digital cities for mobile touch devices in terms of their efficiency and usability, we conducted a user study, where we compared target selection (Go-To), rate control (Joystick), position control, and stroke-based control navigation metaphors. The results suggest users performed best with the Go-To interaction technique. The subjective comments showed a preference of novices towards Go-To and expert users towards Joystick technique.

Article and movie was published at the 9th International IEEE Symposium on 3D User Interfaces (3DUI'14), Minneapolis, USA, 2014.

You can see our software developed for this study on our website 3DIT.

Navidget for Easy 3D Camera Positioning from 2D Inputs

Navidget is a new interaction technique for camera positioning in 3D environments developed by Potioc research team from INRIA Bordeaux Sud-Ouest. Unlike the existing POI techniques, Navidget does not attempt to automatically estimate where and how the user wants to move. Instead, it provides good feedback and control for fast and easy interactive camera positioning. Navidget can also be useful for distant inspection when used with a preview window.

This new 3D User interface is totally based on 2D inputs. As a result, it is appropriate for a wide variety of visualization systems, from small handheld devices to large interactive displays. A user study on Tablet PC shows that the usability of Navidget is very good for both expert and novice users. Apart from these tasks, the Navidget approach can be useful for further purposes such as collaborative work and animation.

CityBird, a Tool for Easy Trajectories Creation in 3D Virtual Cities

CityBird is a tool providing funtionalities for creating quickly and simply trajectories in 3D virtual cities. Always in prototype stage, this tool was developped around a project named Villes Transparentes. Three actors involved in this projet, Mappy, subsidiary of Solocal Group, Vectuel and Potioc research team from INRIA Bordeaux Sud-Ouest. Its goal is to allow users to easily design guided tours on a 3D virtual city. Thereby, it is possible with few clicks to create some complex curves to move 3D objects.

This prototype uses Bezier curves and can be easily extended to other ways.

First movie shows current CityBird prototype, in our workspace. Second movie shows an old version of CityBird but in a more concrete example.

Also you can see a special function named Growing Buildings allowing to simplify the 3D model of our city when camera goes up.

Transition Between 2D and 3D Views in Digital Maps

Here are presented some ideas which simplify understanding and reduces the difficulty of the cognitive task during transition between a 2D map to a 3D virtual urban environment.

You can see the Mantra, a preview window allows us to visualize important landmarks before transition. And the Growing Buildings function, which lightens complexity of our 3D virtual city during transition in order to provide a better understanding of landmarks position.

About Me

My name is Thomas Hulin, I'm a research engineer in Potioc research team from INRIA Bordeaux Sud-Ouest. The focus of the team is Human-computer interaction, and more precisly, interaction in 3D virtual environment. Currently I'm working as an engineer, to maintain and develop 3D softwares. But also as researcher, to find new solutions to problems met in 3D interaction and 3D computer graphics.

I discovered my passion about computer graphics 7 years ago. At the beginning, I was a student in mechanical engineering. I stopped my studies to become banker, but without real convictions. Finally, I decided to go back to university for studying computer science and become a professional in this sector. Now I have my Master degree in computer science with a speciality in computer graphics, with merit. And I still want to improve my skills in this direction that I love so much.
